<p><p>Gratings for space-flight can be categorized into transmission and reflection types. Transmission gratings operate by allowing light to pass through a patterned medium, commonly used in spectrometers to analyze wavelengths in space. Reflection gratings, on the other hand, reflect light from a surface with a specific pattern, making them suitable for applications where minimal light loss is critical. Both types play crucial roles in instrumentation for space missions, enabling precise measurements and observations of celestial phenomena.</p></p>
