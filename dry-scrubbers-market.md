<p><p>Dry scrubbers are advanced air pollution control systems designed to remove harmful pollutants from industrial exhaust streams using dry chemical sorbents. Unlike wet scrubbers, which use water to absorb contaminants, dry scrubbers operate without liquid, making them more suitable for certain applications, especially in environments where water usage is limited. These systems are primarily employed in power plants, cement factories, and other heavy industries where emissions must be controlled to adhere to environmental regulations.</p><p>The Dry Scrubbers Market is witnessing significant growth, driven by increasing regulatory pressures to reduce air pollution and rising awareness regarding environmental sustainability. <p><p>The dry scrubbers market primarily encompasses dry scrubbers and semi-dry scrubbers, each serving to control air pollution. Dry scrubbers utilize a solid absorbent to remove pollutants from exhaust gases, making them ideal for high-temperature applications. In contrast, semi-dry scrubbers combine both dry and wet scrubbing techniques, employing a reagent in a slurry form to capture contaminants. Both technologies are essential in various industries, including power generation and manufacturing, to meet stringent environmental regulations while enhancing air quality.</p></p>
